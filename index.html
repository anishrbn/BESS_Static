The Technology
How Does a BESS Work?

A Battery Energy Storage System stores electrical energy from solar panels or the grid and releases it at the times that save you the most money — automatically, intelligently, around the clock.

☀️
1

Solar Generates Power

Your solar panels produce clean electricity during daylight hours, powering your home directly and reducing grid consumption from the first ray of sunlight.

🔋
2

Battery Stores the Surplus

Any solar energy your property cannot immediately use is stored in your battery rather than wasted through export at low rates. Nothing is lost.

🌙
3

Evening Peak, Zero Cost

When grid electricity is most expensive — typically 4pm–9pm — your battery powers the home. Your evening usage costs you nothing from the grid.

🌐
4

Cheap Overnight Charging

On smart tariffs like Octopus Agile, your battery automatically charges overnight at 4–7p/kWh — a fraction of daytime peak rates.

💷
5

Export & Earn

Surplus energy is exported to the grid under the UK Smart Export Guarantee, earning you 5–15p per kWh — income that compounds your annual savings.

🔌
6

Backup in Power Cuts

Many modern systems include islanding capability — your battery keeps your home powered even when the grid goes down.

🤖 The Intelligence Layer

Your Battery, Guided by AI — Without Any Effort From You

The tariff works best when paired with a compatible smart battery system that uses automated control logic to optimise charging and discharging. This system manages when to charge from the grid or solar panels and when to export energy, aiming to maximise your savings and earnings without manual intervention. The control software is typically included with the battery system and configured by our engineers at installation.

Balanced View
Pros & Cons of Battery Storage

✓ The Advantages

  • Dramatically lower electricity bills — typically 60–80% reduction in grid use
  • Protection against future energy price spikes
  • Earn income via Smart Export Guarantee
  • Backup power during grid outages
  • Reduce your household or business carbon footprint
  • Increase property value and EPC rating
  • Zero VAT on residential installations (since 2023)
  • AI management — no daily manual intervention needed

! Considerations

  • Upfront investment of £5,000–£12,000 for residential systems
  • Payback period typically 6–10 years
  • Battery capacity degrades ~20–30% over 10 years
  • Compatible tariff and inverter needed for full benefit
  • Technology evolving — buy from established brands
  • Some installations may require electrical upgrades
  • Listed buildings may need planning checks
